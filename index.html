The Reality We Face

Imagine a world that is always too loud, too bright, and too fast. For children with autism, this isn't an imagination—it is their daily reality. In Cambodia, awareness of neurodiversity is growing, but resources are lagging behind. Children are placed in mainstream classrooms with no specialized support, expected to simply "cope." We are changing that through our Two-Fold Vision.

1. The Phnom Penh City Sanctuary (Completely Free)

We are building a dedicated Sensory Safe Space and Playground in the heart of Phnom Penh. This community hub will be completely free for all parents of autistic children, providing a safe, professional environment to play and regulate without financial burden.

2. Provincial School Support

We are installing Sensory Rooms in underprivileged provincial schools, equipped with specialized therapeutic tools:

  • Weighted Tools: Blankets and vests for deep pressure.
  • Sensory Equipment: Bubble tubes, fiber-optic lights, and "dark dens."
  • Tactile Toys: Fidget sets and liquid bubblers for self-regulation.
  • Sound Control: Professional noise-canceling headphones.

From Curriculum to Reality

As an Educator and School Principal, I have dedicated my professional life to designing specialized needs curriculums and inclusive education programs. But a curriculum alone isn't enough; a child cannot learn until they first feel secure. I have seen bright children withdraw because they have no place to feel safe.

Through my restaurant, we launched "Nuggets for a Cause." 100% of the profits from nugget sales go directly toward creating safe spaces. Every dollar helps build a more inclusive, compassionate Cambodia.
